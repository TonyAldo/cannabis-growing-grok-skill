# Cannabis Lifecycle: Stage-by-Stage Guide

## Stage 1: Germination (1-5 days)

**Methods (all work, pick your preference):**

- **Paper towel method**: Place seeds between damp (not soaking) paper towels on a plate, cover with another plate, keep in a warm dark spot (75-80°F). Check daily; plant when the taproot is 1/4 to 1/2 inch long.
- **Direct sow**: Plant seed 1/4 to 1/2 inch deep in moist medium, pointed end up (or on its side — it'll figure it out). Keep warm and moist. Slightly slower but avoids the transplant step entirely.
- **Water soak**: Drop seeds in a cup of room-temp water for 12-24 hours (never more than 24 — they can drown). Seeds that sink are generally viable. Transfer to paper towel or medium once they crack.
- **Rapid Rooter / peat pellet**: Place seed in the pre-made hole, keep moist and warm. Good middle ground.

**Tips:**
- Use filtered or pH'd water (6.0-6.5)
- Temperature matters more than people think — below 70°F, germination slows dramatically
- Handle seeds and sprouts gently — the taproot is fragile
- Don't expose the taproot to light
- Old seeds (2+ years) may benefit from a light scuff with sandpaper to help water penetrate the shell

## Stage 2: Seedling (1-3 weeks)

The seedling stage begins when the seed breaks the soil surface with its cotyledons (the small round first "leaves") and ends when the plant has 3-4 sets of true leaves with serrated edges.

**Care:**
- Light: gentle — 200-300 PPFD, 24-30 inches for LED. Seedlings stretch toward light if it's too far and bleach if it's too close.
- Water: very little. The root system is tiny. Water in a small circle around the stem. Overwatering at this stage is the #1 seedling killer.
- Nutrients: none in amended soil. 1/4 strength at most in coco or inert media. Seedlings burn easily.
- Humidity: 65-75% RH. A humidity dome or clear cup over the seedling helps.
- Temperature: 72-80°F (22-27°C)

**Common seedling problems:**
- **Stretching (leggy, tall, thin stem)**: Light is too far away or too weak. Lower the light or increase intensity.
- **Damping off (stem collapses at soil line)**: Fungal — caused by overwatering and poor airflow. Usually fatal. Prevent by not overwatering and keeping airflow gentle but present.
- **Yellowing cotyledons**: Normal after the first set of true leaves develops — the cotyledons are fuel and they get used up.

## Stage 3: Vegetative Growth (2-8+ weeks for photos; 2-4 weeks for autos)

The plant focuses on building structure — stems, branches, leaves, and root mass. This is where training happens.

**Care:**
- Light: ramp up intensity. Target 400-600 PPFD during veg. 18/6 is standard for photos and most autos.
- Water: increase as the plant grows. Water when the top inch of soil is dry, or when the pot feels light. Water to 10-20% runoff to prevent salt buildup.
- Nutrients: full veg formula — higher nitrogen (N), moderate phosphorus (P) and potassium (K). Example NPK ratio: 3-1-2 or similar.
- pH: soil 6.0-6.8; coco/hydro 5.5-6.5
- Humidity: 55-70% RH
- Temperature: 70-82°F (21-28°C)

**Growth rate indicators:**
- Healthy veg growth produces a new node every 1-3 days depending on strain and conditions
- Internodal spacing tells you about light intensity — tight nodes = good light, stretched nodes = needs more light
- Leaf color should be medium to dark green; pale = hungry, very dark with clawing = too much nitrogen

**This is the time to:**
- Top or FIM (photoperiods: anytime; autoflowers: by node 3-4 only if vigorous)
- Start LST
- Set up ScrOG net
- Take clones (photoperiod only)
- Up-pot if needed (photoperiod only — autos should already be in final container)

## Stage 4: Pre-Flower / Transition (1-2 weeks)

### Sexing your plants — identifying male vs. female

This is one of the most critical skills in cannabis growing, especially if running regular (non-feminized) seeds. A single undetected male can pollinate every female in your grow space, filling your buds with seeds and drastically reducing potency and quality. Even with feminized seeds, stress-induced hermaphrodites can appear, so every grower should know how to sex plants.

**When pre-flowers appear:**
- Photoperiod from seed: Pre-flowers can show as early as 4-6 weeks from sprout, even before you flip to 12/12. They become unmistakable within 1-2 weeks of flipping.
- Photoperiod from clone: Clones from a female mother are female — sex is already known.
- Autoflower: Pre-flowers appear naturally around weeks 3-5 without any light change.

**Where to look:** Check the nodes (where branches meet the main stem), especially the upper nodes. Pre-flowers appear in the "V" or crotch between the main stem and a branch. Use a jeweler's loupe or magnifying glass — pre-flowers can be tiny at first.

**Female pre-flowers (what you want):**
- A small pointed calyx (teardrop/pear shape) with one or two white hairs (pistils) emerging from the tip
- The pistils are wispy, hair-like, and white or off-white
- Sometimes described as looking like a tiny "crab claw" with hairs
- Appear at the nodes, usually one per node at first
- As flowering progresses, more pistils emerge and bud sites develop

**Male pre-flowers (remove these):**
- Small round balls (pollen sacs) on short stalks, clustered at the nodes
- Look like tiny bunches of grapes or small smooth balls on stems
- No hairs/pistils — the surface is smooth and rounded
- Males often show sex slightly earlier than females
- As they mature, the sacs open and release fine yellow pollen

**Key visual differences:**
- Female = pointed/teardrop shape + white hairs
- Male = round balls on stalks, no hairs
- If in doubt, wait 2-3 more days — the difference becomes obvious quickly

**What to do with males:**
- Remove them from the grow space immediately. Don't just cut them down in the tent — pollen can release during removal.
- Carefully bag the plant before cutting to contain any pollen, then remove it.
- A single male pollen sac can contain enough pollen to seed multiple females.
- If you're breeding intentionally, isolate the male completely and collect pollen in a controlled way.

**Hermaphrodites (plants with both male and female parts):**
- Can develop male pollen sacs alongside female buds, or produce "nanners" (banana-shaped stamens that emerge from within buds)
- Usually caused by stress: light leaks, heat stress, physical damage, rootbound conditions, or genetics prone to herming
- See the diagnostics reference for detailed hermaphrodite identification and response

**Why feminized seeds exist:** Feminized seeds are bred to produce 99%+ female plants, eliminating the need to sex and cull males. For most home growers, feminized seeds are strongly recommended — they save time, space, and the heartbreak of discovering half your plants are male after weeks of care. Regular seeds are mainly used by breeders and growers doing phenotype hunting.

### Transition care

**Photoperiod:** Triggered by switching to 12/12.

**Autoflower:** Happens naturally around weeks 3-5. You'll see pistils appear at the nodes without any light change.

**Care during transition:**
- Begin shifting nutrients from veg (high N) to bloom (high P-K) over 1-2 weeks
- The "stretch" begins — plants can double in height. Continue LST if needed to manage height.
- Increase light intensity gradually toward flowering levels (600-900 PPFD)
- Watch for hermaphrodites — stress during this transition is the most common trigger

## Stage 5: Flowering (4-12+ weeks depending on strain)

**Early Flower (weeks 1-3):**
- Stretch continues and peaks
- Bud sites form at nodes and branch tips
- White pistils ("hairs") appear abundantly
- Continue transition to full bloom nutrients
- Last chance for light defoliation / lollipopping

**Mid Flower (weeks 3-6):**
- Buds begin to swell and stack
- Trichome production ramps up — plants get frosty
- Terpene production increases — odor management becomes critical (carbon filter)
- Maintain bloom feeding schedule
- Watch for calcium and magnesium demand spikes (especially in coco)
- Lower humidity to 45-55%

**Late Flower (weeks 6-8+):**
- Buds fatten and ripen
- Fan leaves begin to yellow and fade — this is natural nutrient mobilization, not a problem
- Trichomes shift from clear to cloudy to amber — check daily with magnification
- Many growers flush (plain water only) for the final 1-2 weeks. The science is debated but many believe it produces cleaner-burning, smoother flower.
- Lower temperature if possible — 65-75°F days, 55-65°F nights can enhance terpene and anthocyanin (color) expression
- Humidity below 45% — ideally 35-40% to prevent bud rot (Botrytis)

**Flush debate:**
Flushing (running plain water through the medium to remove nutrient buildup) before harvest is controversial. Some studies suggest it doesn't affect mineral content in the final flower. Many experienced growers still do it and report smoother smoke. If in doubt, taper nutrients rather than hard-flushing — reduce feed to 1/4 strength for the final week.

## Stage 6: Harvest

**When to harvest:**
- Primary indicator: trichome color under 60x+ magnification
- Secondary indicator: pistil darkening (70-80% browned/curled)
- Tertiary indicator: fan leaf fade (natural yellowing in late flower)
- See the main SKILL.md trichome guide for cloudy/amber ratios

**How to harvest:**
1. Some growers do a 24-48 hour dark period before harvest (the theory is it boosts resin production — unproven but common practice)
2. Cut the plant at the base, or cut individual branches
3. Optional: remove large fan leaves immediately (wet trim) or leave them on (dry trim)
   - **Wet trim**: easier to trim, faster drying, cleaner look. Risk: can dry too fast.
   - **Dry trim**: slower drying (generally better), leaves protect buds during drying. More work when trimming later.
4. Hang in a drying environment (see drying section in main SKILL.md)

## Stage 7: Drying (7-14 days)

See the main SKILL.md for detailed drying guidance. The key parameters:

- **Temperature**: 60-65°F (15-18°C) — cooler is better for terpene preservation
- **Humidity**: 55-65% RH
- **Airflow**: gentle circulation in the room, never pointed at the plants
- **Light**: dark room — light degrades THC
- **Duration**: 7-14 days. Faster = harsher smoke. The "low and slow" dry is worth the patience.
- **Done when**: small-to-medium stems snap rather than bend; buds feel dry on the outside but not crispy

## Stage 8: Curing (2 weeks to 6+ months)

See the main SKILL.md for detailed curing guidance. Curing is a controlled decomposition process that breaks down chlorophyll (reducing harshness) and allows terpenes to develop. It's the difference between "pretty good weed" and "wow, where did you get this?"

Minimum 2 weeks for acceptable quality. 4-8 weeks is the sweet spot for most strains. Some strains (especially sativas and hazes) continue to improve for 3-6+ months of curing.
