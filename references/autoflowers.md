# Autoflower Cannabis Growing Guide

## What makes autoflowers different

Autoflowers carry Cannabis ruderalis genetics that cause them to flower based on age rather than light cycle. This creates a fundamentally different growing experience from photoperiods.

**Key characteristics:**
- Begin flowering automatically around weeks 3-5 from sprout (strain-dependent)
- Total lifecycle: 60-90 days seed to harvest (some run 100+ days)
- Generally smaller plants (1-4 feet typical), though modern genetics can produce larger specimens
- Can be grown under any light schedule (18/6, 20/4, 24/0 are common)
- Cannot be cloned effectively (clones maintain the mother's age clock)
- Cannot be revegged — once harvested, the plant is done
- Stress recovery window is narrow — mistakes cost more than with photoperiods

## Light schedules for autoflowers

Unlike photoperiods, autoflowers don't need a dark period to flower. Common schedules:

| Schedule | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| 18/6 | Good balance, saves energy, dark period may benefit root growth | Less total light | Most growers, hot climates |
| 20/4 | More light, still gets rest | Higher energy cost | Growers pushing yield |
| 24/0 | Maximum light exposure | Highest energy cost, debated benefits, no rest period | Rare — some growers swear by it |

Most experienced autoflower growers run 18/6 or 20/4. The marginal gains from 24/0 are debatable and the energy cost is real. There's emerging evidence that a dark period benefits auxin redistribution and root development, though it's not conclusive.

## The autoflower timeline

This is approximate — genetics and environment shift things, but it gives a planning framework:

**Week 1 (Days 1-7): Germination & Seedling**
- Seed pops, cotyledons emerge, first true leaves appear
- Very little water needed — keep medium moist but not wet
- No nutrients yet if using pre-amended soil; very light feed (1/4 strength) in coco/inert media
- Light at 24-30 inches (LED), low intensity (~200 PPFD)

**Weeks 2-3 (Days 8-21): Early Veg**
- Plant establishes root system, 3-5 nodes develop
- Begin light LST if desired — this is your window opening
- Start light feeding in soil; ramp to 1/2 strength in coco
- Can top at node 3-4 IF the plant is growing vigorously and healthy — but only if you're comfortable with the risk

**Weeks 3-5 (Days 21-35): Late Veg / Pre-Flower**
- Growth accelerates significantly (the "stretch")
- LST should be active — bend and tuck daily
- Pre-flower pistils appear (small white hairs at nodes)
- Transition nutrients: start reducing N, increasing P-K
- This is the last safe window for any training — stop all HST when pistils show

**Weeks 5-7 (Days 35-49): Early Flower**
- Bud sites form and fill in
- Switch to bloom nutrients (high P-K, lower N)
- Stop all training except gentle leaf tucking
- Begin monitoring trichomes for baseline
- Watch for deficiencies — Cal-Mag demand often spikes here

**Weeks 7-9 (Days 49-63): Mid Flower**
- Buds swell and fatten
- Terpene production intensifies — smell increases significantly
- Maintain bloom feeding, watch for tip burn (sign of excess)
- Begin lowering humidity toward 40-45%
- Consider a light defoliation of any leaves blocking bud sites (be conservative)

**Weeks 9-11+ (Days 63-77+): Late Flower / Ripening**
- Trichomes shift from clear to cloudy to amber
- Many growers begin flushing 1-2 weeks before harvest (with plain water or very light feed)
- Fan leaves start to yellow and fade — this is normal
- Lower temperatures if possible (60-70°F nights) to enhance color and terpene expression
- Harvest when trichomes reach desired cloudiness/amber ratio

## Transplanting autoflowers

**The general rule: plant autoflowers in their final container from the start.** Transplant shock costs recovery time, and autoflowers can't afford to lose days. Start in the final pot (3-5 gallon is typical).

Exceptions where transplanting can work:
- Solo cup → final pot at day 7-10, before roots get rootbound
- If using the exact same medium and you transplant very gently (don't disturb the root ball)
- Some experienced growers transplant from a 1-gallon to a 5-gallon with no issues

If you must transplant: water the source container well, let it sit for an hour, then turn it upside down and let the root ball slide out intact. Place it in a pre-dug hole in the new pot's medium. Don't pack the medium around it tightly.

## Pot size for autoflowers

| Pot Size | Expected Plant Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1-2 gallon | Small, compact | Good for SOG, micro grows |
| 3 gallon | Medium, solid yield | Great all-around size for most autos |
| 5 gallon | Larger plants, higher yield ceiling | Ideal if you have the space |
| 7+ gallon | Diminishing returns for most autos | Usually overkill unless running a long-running sativa auto |

Fabric pots (Smart Pots, Root Pouch) or air pots are strongly recommended — they air-prune roots and prevent circling, promoting a denser root mass.

## Nutrient considerations for autoflowers

Autoflowers are generally more sensitive to nutrient concentration than photoperiods. Start at lower doses and ramp up:

- **Seedling**: No feed in amended soil; 1/4 strength in coco/hydro
- **Early veg**: 1/4 to 1/2 strength
- **Late veg / pre-flower**: 1/2 to 3/4 strength
- **Flower**: 3/4 strength, some strains can handle full strength
- **Late flower**: Taper down; many growers flush with plain water for the last 7-14 days

EC/PPM targets (approximate, strain-dependent):
- Seedling: 0.4-0.6 EC / 200-300 PPM (500 scale)
- Veg: 0.8-1.2 EC / 400-600 PPM
- Flower: 1.2-1.8 EC / 600-900 PPM
- Late flower: 0.6-1.0 EC / 300-500 PPM

Watch the plant, not the chart. Burnt tips = back off. Pale/hungry = feed more.

## Common autoflower mistakes

1. **Overwatering seedlings** — Tiny plant in a big pot means the soil stays wet for days. Water in a small circle around the seedling, not the whole pot.
2. **Topping too late** — If you're going to top, do it by node 3-4 while still in veg. Topping after pre-flower is a recipe for stunted plants.
3. **Transplant shock** — Start in the final pot. If you must transplant, do it very early and very gently.
4. **Over-feeding** — Autos need less food than photos. Start at 1/4-1/2 strength and work up.
5. **Too much training in flower** — Once an auto is flowering, it's building buds, not recovering from stress. Keep hands off except for gentle leaf tucking.
6. **Expecting photo-sized yields** — A healthy auto yields 1-4 oz typically. Exceptional runs can hit 6+ oz but that's not the norm. Set realistic expectations.
7. **Running the wrong light schedule** — 12/12 wastes potential on autos. They don't need the dark to flower. Run 18/6 minimum.
